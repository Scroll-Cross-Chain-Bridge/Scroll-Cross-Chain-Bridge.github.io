What is Scroll Cross Chain Bridge?

Scroll is a zkEVM Layer 2 network for Ethereum, and the official portal provides the guided route for moving supported assets between Ethereum and Scroll. Review the official bridge docs before signing.

Scroll cross chain bridge fees and gas

Scroll bridge fees depend mainly on Ethereum gas, Scroll gas, token approval needs, and the direction of the transfer. Depositing from Ethereum to Scroll typically requires an Ethereum transaction, so mainnet gas can dominate the cost. Moving back to Ethereum can involve L2-to-L1 messaging and finalization mechanics. The bridge page should be treated as a live quote screen: check token, amount, chain direction, gas, and wallet prompt before signing. Generic fee claims are less useful than the current transaction estimate in the official portal.

Scroll bridge withdrawal time to Ethereum

Withdrawing from Scroll to Ethereum is not always as fast as depositing into Scroll because L2-to-L1 exits can require protocol finalization. The exact user experience depends on the official portal flow, network status, and the asset being withdrawn. Users should avoid assuming that a Scroll-to-Ethereum transfer is instant just because a previous Ethereum-to-Scroll deposit was fast. Track the transaction status in the portal and use Scrollscan or an Ethereum explorer to confirm each stage.

Scroll cross chain bridge supported assets

The official Scroll bridge is focused on moving supported assets between Ethereum and Scroll. Common bridge demand includes ETH and major ERC-20 assets, but the final list should be checked inside the portal because token support can change. A token symbol alone is not enough; users should verify the contract, source network, destination representation, and amount. If an asset is unavailable in the canonical portal, a third-party bridge may offer a route, but that adds provider-specific risk.

Scroll bridge transaction pending or stuck

A Scroll bridge transaction can appear pending while the source-chain transaction confirms, the portal indexes status, or an L2-to-L1 withdrawal progresses. Start with the transaction hash on the source explorer. If the transaction reverted, the bridge did not complete even though gas may have been spent. If it succeeded, check the portal status and destination explorer before retrying. For support, keep the transaction hash, source chain, destination chain, token, amount, and wallet address.

Scroll Cross Chain Bridge FAQ

What is the Scroll cross chain bridge?

The Scroll cross chain bridge is the official portal route for moving supported assets between Ethereum and Scroll, an Ethereum Layer 2 network. Users connect a wallet, choose a direction, select a token and amount, then sign the transaction. The bridge does not remove normal smart-contract, gas, or wallet-signing risk.

What is the official Scroll bridge URL?

The official bridge route is portal.scroll.io/bridge. Users should verify the portal.scroll.io domain before connecting a wallet. Fake bridge sites often imitate Layer 2 projects, so domain verification, wallet prompt review, and token contract checks are important before signing.

How long does it take to bridge to Scroll?

Ethereum-to-Scroll deposits can settle after the required source-chain confirmation and portal processing, while Scroll-to-Ethereum withdrawals can take longer because they follow L2-to-L1 finalization mechanics. The portal status and transaction hashes are the best way to track timing for a specific transfer.

What tokens can I bridge to Scroll?

The official portal supports selected assets between Ethereum and Scroll. ETH and major ERC-20 assets are common user targets, but the live bridge interface is the final source for supported tokens. Always verify token contracts and destination representation before approving a transfer.

Why is my Scroll bridge withdrawal not showing on Ethereum?

A withdrawal may still be moving through L2-to-L1 finalization or waiting for a claim step. Check the source transaction on Scrollscan, then follow the portal's status for the withdrawal. Do not repeat the withdrawal until you know whether the first one is pending, claimable, reverted, or complete.

Does Scroll bridge require ETH for gas?

Yes. Users generally need ETH for gas on Ethereum and ETH on Scroll for Scroll-side transactions. Token approvals may also cost gas. Before bridging, keep enough native gas on the source network and, when needed, on the destination network for follow-up actions.
